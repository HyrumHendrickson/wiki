Summary EduWiki is an interactive educational resource built to make learning engaging and accessible. This guide will help you navigate the site, find articles, and make the most of its interactive features like Desmos graphs, dropdown sections, and LaTeX math.

What is EduWiki?

EduWiki is a modular, Wikipedia-style educational wiki. Unlike traditional references that just list facts, EduWiki is designed to teach. Articles feature:

  • Summaries ‚Äî a quick overview at the top of every page.
  • Structured sections ‚Äî concepts built up step by step.
  • Interactive Desmos graphs ‚Äî explore math visually.
  • LaTeX math ‚Äî beautifully typeset equations.
  • Expandable examples ‚Äî click to reveal worked problems.
  • Citations ‚Äî sources and further reading at the bottom.

Navigating the Site

The Header

The header at the top of every page contains:

  • The EduWiki logo ‚Äî clicking it always takes you to the home page.
  • The search bar ‚Äî type to find articles instantly.
  • Quick links to Home and Help.

The Sidebar

On article pages, the left sidebar shows:

  • The Table of Contents ‚Äî click any heading to jump to it. The current section is highlighted as you scroll.
  • The article navigation ‚Äî all articles grouped by category.

Search

Use the search bar in the header (or the large search box on the home page) to find articles. Results appear as you type. Press Enter to go to the best match, or click any result in the dropdown.

Reading an Article

Each article follows the same structure:

  1. Breadcrumb navigation ‚Äî shows where you are.
  2. Title & metadata ‚Äî article name, author, last updated date, tags.
  3. Summary box (blue) ‚Äî a brief overview of the topic.
  4. Info box (right column) ‚Äî key facts at a glance.
  5. Main sections ‚Äî the teaching content.
  6. Interactive components ‚Äî graphs, examples, dropdowns.
  7. References ‚Äî citations and further reading.

Interactive Features

Many math articles embed live Desmos graphing calculators. You can:

  • Click and drag to pan the graph.
  • Scroll to zoom in and out.
  • Click the expression list icon (top-left of the graph) to see and edit equations.
  • Interact with sliders to explore how changing a value affects the graph.

Equations are rendered using MathJax, which supports full LaTeX syntax. Inline math like $E = mc^2$ appears in the flow of text. Display math appears centered on its own line:

$$\int_0^\infty e^{-x^2}\,dx = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}$$

Blue dropdown sections (like this one!) contain worked examples, proofs, or extra detail. Click the button to expand or collapse them. This keeps the main article clean while providing depth for those who want it.
